Eric Mack Johnson
A social platform and CRM for online education

User research • Wireframes • Mockups • Prototypes • Usability studies • Front-end  development • A/B testing • Analytics

Purpose

Online education had become a powerful democratizing force, but attrition rates were high and student engagement low

Fidelis set out to create a tool to organize online education around goals, positive social pressures, and giving the student control of their own experience. Their product allows students to take online classes as a part of their personalized learning plan, while teachers and faculty help them achieve their goals.




Target Audiences

1. Students taking classes online

2. Teachers and faculty tracking student progress

3. Teachers building online learning experiences

4. Hiring managers looking for new employees

PROCESS

1. Research

We talked to American Public University System employees about the most critical points in their workflow

We learned teachers were having a hard time tracking at-risk students and making sure they don't fall through the cracks. We also found it was important to give students a personalized learning plan so all their classes were working towards and overall goal. And yes, sometimes meetings included babies and dogs.

2. Wireframes & team feedback

We reviewed sketched wireframes of the application internally

I sketched out user flows for students taking online classes and teachers, faculty, and mentors tracking their progress. I presented the wireframes to our team and made changes based on their feedback.

3. Mockups & client feedback

We presented desktop and mobile mockups to APUS staff and made changes based on their feedback

I created mockups in Photoshop and used Google Hangouts to present the different experiences to APUS stakeholders. They provided me with their key metrics and indicators and we adjusted the interface to meet their needs.

4. Development & QA

I traveled to Pune, India and worked with our engineering team to turn the designs into working code

We had worked remotely with our engineering team up until this point, but for this development sprint, we thought it would be essential to work together in-person. This allowed us to communicate clearly and build the collective ownership of our goals. I spent a month working in their office, helping engineers interpret the mockups, editing CSS, and testing early versions of the product on different devices and browser versions.

5. Results & follow-up

APUS staff now use the Learning Relationship Manager to track the success of over 20,000 students

After the development and QA process was complete, we onboarded APUS students onto the site and they used our technology to manage the entirety of their online educaiton experience. We set up regular meetings with staff and stakeholders to get feedback and make adjustments as students progressed.
