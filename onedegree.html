Purpose

One Degree had built a best-in-class social service database, but users were having a hard time navigating the site

One Degree's mission is to connect people with the resources they need to overcome poverty. When I joined, they had already made incredible progress with just the two co-founders. My job was to make their social service data accessible by anyone, anywhere – from a smartphone to a library computer.




Target Audiences

1. Families searching for social services

2. Social workers referring resources to their clients and tracking their status

3. Service providers listing their resources and tracking client status

PROCESS

1. Research

We ran a usability study to see what the biggest pain-points were in the user experience

I used the live site to conduct usability studies with 5 community members and used the results to make a priotized plan for improving the user experience. We immediately made a few changes to the existing site, then started preparting for a design overhaul.

2. Identifying the user funnel

I conducted a team session to map out the touchpoints and interactions with users

To better understand all the ways One Degree interacted with different audiences, I created a user experience map of how users were introduced to the organization, solved a problem or got value from the interaction, and then repeated this for a long-term relationship. This led to a key new metric: The Indicator of Access, or proof that a member had accessed services.

3. Wireframes & mockups

I presented wireframes and mockups of the site to our team and iterated after each round of feedback

With the results of the usability study and a new understanding of our user funnel and goals, I drew wireframes, built mockups in Sketch, and walked our team through the different experiences. After a few rounds of refinement, the engineers began coding the back-end while I prepared to get external feedback.

4. Prototype & usability studies

We went to community events to do user testing with a clickable prototype of the new designs

I used InVision to create clickable prototypes of the mockups, both mobile and desktop, and drafted questions for a usability study. I met with 5 community members and 5 nonprofit professionals and asked them to complete different tasks on the prototype. I noted where they were successful and where they encountered difficulties.

5. Development & QA

I used the prototypes to communicate the designs to the engineers and guide the QA process

After making improvements from the usability studies, I created internal prototypes to communicate different user flows to the engineering team. I also made a CSS guide that would help us create a consistent front-end style. When they had made a first pass, I helped refine the CSS and tested the key user flowers on different browser versions and devices.

6. Launch

We invited social workers and community members from all over the Bay Area for a launch party at our office

We used social media, email campaigns, and blog posts to announce our new site and gathered to celebrate. Nonprofit professionals from Contra Costa shared how helpful the site would be for their remote clients.

7. Results & follow-up

Over 8,000 people now rely on One Degree to access resources every month

In the year we launched the new site, One Degree's membership doubled from 3,356 to 6,6602 members. 91,906 unique people used the site, a 139% increase from the previous year. We continued to add and refine features, like a new referral tool for case managers.
